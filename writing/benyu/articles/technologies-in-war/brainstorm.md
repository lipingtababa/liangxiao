## Key Finding 6: Close-Range Direct Fire vs. Long-Range Indirect Fire

ðŸ’¡ **Zhu Rui's 4th principle (close-range direct fire) was tactical innovation born from equipment limitations**

### The Tactical Distinction:

**Direct Fire (Close Range):**
- Shooting directly at visible target within line-of-sight
- Typically within 2,000 meters
- **High risk:** "If you are firing direct fire with artillery, things have gotten bad quick - it's your last defensive line"
- **High reward:** Can precisely destroy fortified positions in urban warfare
Source: [Direct fire - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_fire)

**Indirect Fire (Long Range):**
- Principal method of modern artillery support
- "Fire delivered at a target which cannot be seen by the aimer"
- Can shoot over obstacles, weapons concealed from counter-battery fire
- Became primary method after WWI
Source: [Indirect fire - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indirect_fire)

### Why PLA Used Direct Fire:

**Equipment Constraint:** Captured Japanese artillery + lack of advanced targeting systems â†’ couldn't do sophisticated indirect fire

**Tactical Innovation:** Made it a principle rather than a limitation
- "Close-range shooting and direct aiming" (è¿‘è·ç¦»ç›´çž„å°„å‡»)
- Combined with "fast, accurate, and fierce"
- Turned disadvantage into doctrine

### Battle of Jinzhou Demonstration (Oct 14-15, 1948):

**Artillery Concentration:**
- Sources vary: 500, 591, or 900 artillery pieces (number discrepancy itself interesting!)
- **Main tactic:** Direct bombardment of city walls
- **Result:** Breached Jinzhou's city walls "in just a few hours"
- City captured in 31 hours, 80,000 Nationalist troops captured
Source: [Battle of Jinzhou - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Jinzhou)

**Strategic Impact:**
- Mao Zedong: "Key to success of entire Liaoshen Campaign is to capture Jinzhou in one week"
