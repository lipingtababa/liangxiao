## ğŸ’¡ Finding 1: ChatGPT Usage Patterns - The Three Activity Types

Source: [How People Use ChatGPT - OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/how-people-are-using-chatgpt/)

OpenAI identified three core usage categories:
- **Asking (49% of activity)**: Users seek information, advice, and factual answers
- **Doing (40% of activity)**: Task-oriented work including writing emails, coding, planning projects, and generating content
- **Expressing (11% of activity)**: Personal reflection, creative experiments, and exploratory conversations without specific goals

**Why this is interesting**: This maps to funnel levels 4-6. Level 4 (just asking ChatGPT) = "Asking". Level 6 (hands-on experimentation) = "Doing" + "Expressing". Most users stuck at level 4 are only using 49% of AI's potential.

**Provocative angle**: The funnel is actually about transitioning from **passive consumption** (Asking) to **active production** (Doing/Expressing).

Sources:

**Provocative insight**: The real barrier between level 4 and level 6 is **admitting the AI output isn't good enough**. People at level 4 are in denial about the quality of their AI-assisted work.

## ğŸ’¡ Finding 8: The Feynman Technique - Why Level 6â†’7 is Critical

Sources:
- [Feynman Technique - Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/feynman-technique/)
- [Feynman Technique - University of York](https://subjectguides.york.ac.uk/study-revision/feynman-technique)
- [Feynman Technique - Oxford Learning](https://oxfordlearning.com/the-feynman-technique-study-skills-secret-weapon/)

**Four-step process**:
1. Study the concept
2. **Teach it simply** (as if to a sixth-grader)
3. **Identify gaps** - things you can't explain simply reveal your gaps
4. **Review and simplify** - refine until you can explain simply AND accurately

**Why it works**:
- "The more you teach a subject, the better you grasp it"
- Teaching forces you to identify where you're using jargon to **mask lack of understanding**
- If you can't explain simply, **you don't truly understand it**

**Why this validates the funnel's level 7**:
- Level 6 = self-experimentation (step 1-2 of Feynman)
- Level 7 = public teaching + iteration (steps 3-4 repeated publicly)
- The gap identification (step 3) REQUIRES external feedback, which only happens by going public

**Provocative angle**: Most people THINK they understand AI because they can use it (level 4-5). But if you can't **teach it clearly** (level 7), you don't actually understand it. Level 7 is the **litmus test** for true mastery.

## ğŸ’¡ Finding 9: Growth Mindset and Feedback Loops

Sources:
- [Carol Dweck Revisits Growth Mindset - EdWeek](https://www.edweek.org/leadership/opinion-carol-dweck-revisits-the-growth-mindset/2015/09)
- [Growth Mindset Summary - Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/carol-dweck-mindset/)

**Core concepts**:
- **Growth mindset**: Belief that capacities can be developed over time
- **Fixed mindset**: Belief that capacities are inherent and unchangeable
- Students with growth mindset **outperform** those with fixed mindset

**Feedback loop mechanics**:
- Growth mindset = seeing feedback as **opportunity to learn**
- Students need **positive reinforcement + constructive feedback** to improve
- Must try **new strategies** and **seek input** when stuck - not just effort

**Critical insight from recent research**:
- Growth mindset can create "potentially endless feedback loops with no exit conditions"
- Need **recursive feedback loops** that allow for self-evaluation of when to pivot vs. persist

**Mapping to the funnel**:
- Level 1-3: **Fixed mindset** about AI ("it's just a fad," "old methods are better")
- Level 4-5: **Transitional** - trying AI but not seeking feedback
- Level 6: **Growth mindset** activated - self-experimentation with internal feedback
- Level 7: **Recursive growth mindset** - public feedback + iteration + self-evaluation

**Provocative question**: Is the funnel really about AI adoption, or is it about **developing growth mindset** in a new domain?
